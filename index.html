How a Meeting Works

▼
A typical Toastmasters meeting has four main parts:
1. Pathways Project Speeches — Members deliver speeches based on their Pathways projects. The goal isn't just the speech — it's achieving the project objectives (storytelling, persuasion, body language, etc.). Each speech is followed by an individual evaluation.
2. Table Topics — Impromptu speaking! A Table Topics Master gives random prompts, and speakers respond for 1–2 minutes with no preparation. Great for thinking on your feet.
3. Sharing Session — Members or guests share professional or Toastmasters-related topics with the club. A chance to practice presenting ideas, expose ourselves to new perspectives, and learn from each other.
4. Evaluations & Reports — The General Evaluator leads the feedback session. Timer, Ah-Counter, and Grammarian give their reports. Everyone learns from everyone.
Every meeting role — from Timer to Toastmaster of the Day — is filled by members, so everyone practices leadership and communication skills every single meeting.

Pathways: Your Learning Journey

▼
Pathways is Toastmasters' education program. You pick a path, complete projects through speeches and leadership activities, and level up through 5 levels.
5 Levels — Each level builds on the last: Mastering Fundamentals → Learning Your Style → Increasing Knowledge → Building Skills → Demonstrating Expertise
14 Projects per Path — A mix of required and elective projects. Each project has a specific skill focus, evaluation criteria, and resources.

Club Officer Roles

▼
Every Toastmasters club is run by elected member officers. Each role is a leadership opportunity:
President
Leads the club, sets direction, chairs business meetings, and represents the club at area/division events.
VP Education (VPE)
Plans the meeting schedule, assigns speakers and roles, tracks member progress, and ensures educational quality.
VP Membership (VPM)
Welcomes guests, manages the membership pipeline, conducts interviews, and handles onboarding of new members.
VP Public Relations (VPPR)
Promotes the club externally — social media, flyers, partnerships, and community outreach to attract guests.
Secretary
Takes meeting minutes, manages club records, handles correspondence, and maintains the club roster.
Treasurer
Manages club finances — dues collection, expense tracking, budgeting, and financial reporting to the club.
Sergeant at Arms (SAA)
Sets up the meeting venue, manages equipment and supplies, greets guests, and ensures the room is ready.
Officer terms run July–June. Serving as an officer counts toward Pathways leadership projects and is required for the Distinguished Toastmaster (DTM) award.
